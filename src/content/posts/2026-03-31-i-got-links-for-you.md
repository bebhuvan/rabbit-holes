There's de-dollarization in the underground economy, and the dollar is losing ground to cryptocurrencies. [From this interview with Ken Rogoff](https://amp-scmp-com.cdn.ampproject.org/v/s/amp.scmp.com/economy/china-economy/article/3348156/why-kenneth-rogoff-thinks-chinas-yuan-will-be-reserve-currency-next-5-years?amp_gsa=1&amp_js_v=a9&usqp=mq331AQIUAKwASCAAgM%3D#amp_ct=1774922850183&amp_tf=From%20%251%24s&aoh=17749228469298&referrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.google.com&ampshare=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.scmp.com%2Feconomy%2Fchina-economy%2Farticle%2F3348156%2Fwhy-kenneth-rogoff-thinks-chinas-yuan-will-be-reserve-currency-next-5-years) on the dollar, yuan, and more..

> Cryptocurrency already is a major challenger to the US dollar in the global underground economy. The underground economies include human trafficking, drugs and illegal arms sales, but mainly tax evasion. And in my book, I estimated the world’s underground economy to be roughly 20 per cent of the total global economy. That is a big number. That is at least US$20 trillion.
>
> And cryptocurrency already is very competitive for illicit transactions, where the US dollar was once the undisputed king. People were always using suitcases of US$100 bills to pay for houses, to pay for drugs, and so forth. Now, there are many papers showing that cryptocurrency, particularly stablecoins, has become very important. So cryptocurrency is already taking significant market shares from the US dollar in the underground economy.
