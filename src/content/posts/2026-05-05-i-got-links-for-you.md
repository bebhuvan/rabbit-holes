> While leadership sleeps four hours a night generating 37,000 lines of bloated code, the New York Times coined a term for what’s happening downstream: “++[tokenmaxxing](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/20/technology/tokenmaxxing-ai-agents.html)++.” It’s a competitive status game where employees race to consume the most AI tokens. OpenAI has an engineer who processed 210 billion tokens in a single week. Anthropic has a single Claude Code user running a $150,000 monthly bill. Shopify’s Tobi Lutke made AI usage a ++[factor in performance reviews](https://www.fastcompany.com/91312832/shopify-ceo-tobi-lutke-ai-is-now-a-fundamental-expectation-for-employees)++ (Meta did the same). Some companies have literal internal leaderboards tracking who burns the most tokens.
>
> The leaderboard measures consumption, not output.
